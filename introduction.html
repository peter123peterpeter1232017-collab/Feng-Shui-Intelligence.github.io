Understanding Feng Shui

Ancient wisdom meets modern technology

What is Feng Shui?

Feng Shui (pronounced "fung shway") is an ancient Chinese practice that aims to harmonize individuals with their surrounding environment. The term literally translates to "wind" (feng) and "water" (shui), representing the flow of energy or "chi" through a space.

Dating back over 3,000 years, Feng Shui is based on the Taoist understanding of nature and the belief that our living spaces reflect and affect our inner lives. By arranging our environments according to Feng Shui principles, we can create balance and harmony that supports our health, wealth, relationships, and overall well-being.

Based on traditional Feng Shui principles using the ancient Chinese compass (Luo Pan)

Core Principles of Feng Shui

Understanding the fundamental concepts that guide this ancient practice

Yin and Yang

The balance of opposing yet complementary forces. In Feng Shui, spaces should have a harmonious balance of yin (passive, dark, soft) and yang (active, bright, hard) elements.

Chi (Energy Flow)

The vital life force that flows through everything. Good Feng Shui creates spaces where chi can flow smoothly without stagnating or rushing too quickly.

Five Elements

Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, and Water represent different types of energy. Balancing these elements in your space creates harmony and supports different aspects of life.

Bagua Map

An energy map that divides spaces into nine areas, each corresponding to different life aspects such as wealth, family, knowledge, career, and relationships.

Command Position

Placing important furniture (bed, desk, stove) in positions of power and security, typically diagonally from the door with a solid wall behind for support.

Balance and Harmony

Creating environments that feel balanced in terms of color, texture, light, space, and sound to promote overall well-being and positive energy.
